Computing Programming

Computing Programming immerses students in the world of Python programming. Through this subject, I delved into Procedural Programming, fundamental Python concepts, and Computational Thinking. Additionally, we honed our skills in utilizing Integrated Development Environments (IDEs), pivotal platforms for writing and executing code.

The highlight of this course was undoubtedly the creation of various programs, ranging from a cash machine simulator to an engaging Q&A game. The boundless creativity afforded by this subject makes it my absolute favorite, offering endless opportunities for innovation and exploration.

Object-Oriented Prorgamming

Object-Oriented Programming (OOP) introduces students to a programming paradigm centered around objects and data, contrasting with the procedural approach focused on functions and logic. Widely regarded as the dominant programming paradigm today, OOP's emphasis on real-world abstraction makes it immensely popular.

Personally, I'm drawn to OOP not only for its practical relevance but also for its utilization of Python, a language I deeply enjoy. The fusion of theoretical concepts with hands-on Python programming makes OOP a captivating and rewarding subject for me.
